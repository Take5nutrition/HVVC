About Us

Why We Built HVVC.

We are proud to be the home of HVVC - a growing competitive club volleyball program, as well as The HVVC Academy, our seasonal training program for athletes of all levels.

Happy Valley Volleyball Club is built on a passion for the sport and a deep commitment to giving back to the community of youth volleyball. We focus on the athlete in front of us and build teams that play fast, communicate well, and understand why each drill is on the plan.

We keep our operations intentionally small so coaches can know every athlete personally, delivering high-quality skill instruction, positive culture, and real development opportunities for local athletes.

“We believe every athlete deserves a coach who knows their name, understands their goals, and pushes them to be better every day.”

Foundation

What We
Stand For

The principles that guide everything we do - from the first practice to the final tournament.

To develop confident, competitive athletes through intentional coaching, positive culture, and a commitment to every player's growth - on and off the court. We believe finances should never keep an athlete off the court, which is why HVVC operates as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit.

To be the most intentional, athlete-centered volleyball club in the Pacific Northwest - known for developing players who compete with confidence, lead with character, and love the game for life.

Athletes thrive in environments where they feel supported, celebrated, and safe to fail forward. We build that environment every single day - in practice, at tournaments, and beyond the court.

Every practice plan prioritizes high-quality repetitions, real-time feedback, and application to live play. No wasted time, no wasted reps. Our coaches prepare structured sessions with clear skill progressions.

Clear communication about calendars, events, expectations, and growth pathways. Families always know what's happening and why. We compete hard, but we build harder.

2+ monthly tournaments with professional coaching at every level. We compete hard, learn from every set, and grow as a program. Power League Series, friendlies, and regionals.
